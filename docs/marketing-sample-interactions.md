### Correct answer feedback
> "Correct! The blade inlet angle is calculated from the relative velocity components at entry. The whirl component of the absolute velocity minus the blade speed gives you the relative whirl, and the ratio with the flow component gives you the angle — that's the core of the velocity triangle. *Question 2 of 8 is ready — give it your best shot.*"

### Wrong answer feedback

## Notes for image production

- The **debrief** is the most powerful image — shows personalized adaptive intelligence in one glance
- The **exam opening** paired with the chapter/progress indicator shows the exam-mode UI clearly
- For quiz images, consider showing the question card + one of the feedback messages side by side
- Student name can be swapped for any name that photographs well (e.g. "Alex", "Jordan", "Chris")
- Bold text in the responses renders as-is in the agent chat panel
